University Wide Initiative: TrojanSecure App

Spearheaded the development of TrojanSecure app proof-of-concept designed to elevate information security awareness at USC. Integrating the 'Have I Been Pwned' API, TrojanSecure alerts users about data breaches and offers interactive learning through quizzes and gamification to promote continual engagement in cybersecurity practices. Tailored for USC faculty, students, and staff, the app also provides timely notifications about cybersecurity events and webinars, ensuring the community remains informed and vigilant. Development of this groundbreaking app will continue in the upcoming semester.

Springer Publication: A Study on Smart Contract Security Vulnerabilities

Blockchain enables secure, decentralized communication and transactions without a central authority by leveraging concepts such as decentralization, immutability, and consensus. This research paper explores various categories of vulnerabilities in blockchain technology, including Denial of Service with Failed Call, Randomness using ‘Block Hash’, and Transaction Ordering Dependency. It also discusses preventive measures and detection tools to enhance blockchain security, with a focus on the emerging application of Smart Contracts.
